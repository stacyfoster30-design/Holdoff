Why Does He Take Hours to Text Back?

The real answer — not just the one that makes you feel better.

You sent the text. You watched it deliver. Two hours later — nothing.

So you check. Put the phone down. Check again. Tell yourself you're not checking. Check one more time.

5 actual reasons he hasn't replied

1. He's genuinely not on his phone. Men check their phones less frequently than women on average. He might be in a meeting, at the gym, or just not in texting mode right now.

2. His response anxiety is lower than yours. If you have anxious attachment, you feel slow responses as a threat signal. He probably doesn't register time the same way at all.

3. He doesn't know it matters to you. Unless you've told him, he has zero idea a two-hour gap is causing you distress.

4. He's thinking about what to say. Some people — especially more avoidant types — sit with a message before responding. They're not ignoring you. They're processing.

5. He is pulling back. Yes, sometimes. But this is one of five reasons, not the first one — even though your anxiety always jumps there first.

What NOT to send while you wait

  • A follow-up "did you get this?" — he got it
  • A casual "lol anyway" to seem unbothered — it doesn't land that way
  • That message you've been rewriting for 45 minutes — seriously, don't

What actually helps

Put your phone in a different room. Set a 30-minute rule. Do literally anything else.
