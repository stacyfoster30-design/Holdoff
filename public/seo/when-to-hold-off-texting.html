When to Hold Off Texting

Some texts should never be sent. Here's when.

The Situations That Always Backfire

Not all texts are equal. Some are fine at any hour, in any mood. Others — sent at the wrong moment, in the wrong state — reliably create the thing you were trying to prevent.

Hold Off When You're In the Feeling

If you're crying, shaking, heart racing, or typing with your chest rather than your head — you're in the feeling. The message you send from inside the feeling almost never represents what you actually want to communicate. It represents the feeling. And feelings are temporary; texts are permanent.

Hold Off at 11pm and Later

Night is its own category. Emotional regulation is lowest. Stakes feel highest. Everything that's slightly uncomfortable becomes urgent at night. If the conversation can wait until morning, it should.

Hold Off After More Than Two Attempts

If you've sent two unanswered messages, a third one is rarely about communication anymore. It's about managing the anxiety that the silence is creating. That's a problem that no reply will actually solve.

Hold Off Before You Know What You Want

Sometimes a message opens a conversation you're not actually ready for. If you don't know what you want from the exchange — what you're hoping they say, what a good outcome looks like — you're probably not ready to start it yet.

Common Questions

What's the ideal time to send an emotional text?

When you're regulated — not necessarily calm, but not in the acute spike. Most emotional texts that people regret were sent during the spike. Most that they're glad they sent were sent after it.

How long should I wait before sending?

As a rule of thumb: if you wrote it in the last 20 minutes and you're in the feeling, wait. If it still seems right in the morning or after 30 minutes of not looking at it — it's probably a reasonable send.

What if waiting means they think I don't care?

Most people can't actually tell the difference between a message sent at the moment of the feeling and one sent an hour later. You can. They usually can't.
