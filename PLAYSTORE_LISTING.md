## Full Description
Understand yourself. Protect your relationships.

Every message tells a story—not just about what's happening between you two, but about what's happening inside *you*.

HoldOff is your AI safety net for relationships. It analyzes every message before you send it, helping you understand the patterns behind your words: the anxious texting, the walls you build, the moments you reach out when you're really hurting.

**What HoldOff Does:**
- **Verdict** — Real-time message analysis. Is this safe to send? What are you really feeling?
- **Interpreter** — Decode incoming messages. What's *really* being said? What patterns do you see?
- **Spiral Lock** — Stop the 3am spiral. Pause the impulse. Understand the urge.
- **AI Companion** — Meet Stacy & Danny. Two characters with different attachment styles who understand what you're going through. Ask them anything. They'll help you see yourself more clearly.

**Your Personal Story:**
Watch Stacy and Danny's story—and then step into their shoes to understand your own. Every insight is personalized to *your* attachment style, relationship patterns, and the specific conditions you're navigating (anxiety, RSD, ADHD, depression, addiction, autism, and more).

**Who This Is For:**
- People with anxious attachment who struggle with text analysis
- Anyone dealing with RSD (Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria)
- People with ADHD, autism, or anxiety who need clarity before hitting send
- Anyone trying to break unhealthy relationship patterns
- Couples working to understand each other better

**How It Works:**
1. Sign up. Tell us about yourself and your attachment style.
2. Start texting. HoldOff analyzes in real-time.
3. Get insights. Understand your patterns.
4. Meet your companion. Chat with Stacy or Danny about what you're feeling.
5. Transform. Real understanding leads to real change.

**Important:**
HoldOff is not therapy, not a diagnosis, and not a substitute for professional mental health care. It's a tool for self-awareness and relationship clarity. If you're in crisis, please reach out to a mental health professional.
